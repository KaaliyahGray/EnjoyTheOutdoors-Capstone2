Mount Everest

Mount Everest, also known in Nepali as Sagarmatha and in Tibetan as Chomolungma, is Earth's highest mountain above sea level, located in the Mahalangur Himal sub-range of the Himalayas. Its peak is 8,848.86 meters (29,031.7 feet) above sea level.

K2

K2, also known as Mount Godwin-Austen or Chhogori, is the second-highest mountain in the world, after Mount Everest, at 8,611 meters (28,251 feet) above sea level.

Kangchenjunga

Kangchenjunga is the third highest mountain in the world, with an elevation of 8,586 meters (28,169 feet) and lies partly in Nepal and partly in the Indian state of Sikkim.

Lhotse

Lhotse is the fourth highest mountain in the world at 8,516 meters (27,940 feet) above sea level. It is located at the border between Tibet (China) and the Khumbu region of Nepal.
